SOMA by example: simulating characters that reveal themselves

A complete, self-contained tutorial for the soma.narrative library — writing simulated people whose bodies tell on them, and reading the novelistic insight back out. Every line of code below is quoted from a runnable file, and every block of output was produced by running that code; this document is generated by build_tutorial.py, which captures both live, so nothing here can be stale.

What this is

SOMA is a small language for simulating the mind–body loop of a character. You do not write what a character does; you write how they are built — what they sense, what their body makes of it, what they feel, and the story they tell themselves about it. Then you run the simulation and read what happened.

The payoff is a particular kind of insight. Because a character's narrator (the voice in their head) and their body are separate mechanisms, they can disagree — and that gap, logged event by event, is exactly the stuff of novelistic characterization: the person who says I'm fine while their heart races; the value held in words and broken in the flesh; the feeling that never quite reaches awareness; the hunger the world keeps feeding and wiping.

This tutorial starts from a bare shell prompt and builds up to a whole novel's cast. It is organized simple → complex, and every section shows you three things: the code (what you wrote), the output (what running it produced), and an explanation tying specific lines of output back to the code and out to what they mean about the character.

You need no prior familiarity with the base SOMA language; the narrative library sits on top of it, and we look at the generated SOMA only once, to prove there is no magic underneath.

1. Getting it running

Everything lives in one Python package, soma, with a high-level authoring layer at soma.narrative. You need Python 3.10+ and, for the sparkline plots in the dashboard, numpy.

Unzip the project and step inside:

unzip soma_lang.zip
cd soma_lang

Confirm the engine works by running the test suite — it should report all tests passing:

python3 -m pytest -q

There are two ways to drive the library.

The Python front end. You write a Story, call methods on it to build up characters, place a few events in time, and print a view. Save this as my_first.py anywhere inside the project folder:

import sys
sys.path.insert(0, ".")          # so `import soma` finds the package
from soma.narrative import Story, anxious

story = Story("first_feeling", span="6s", step="1s", about="a first feeling")
mara = story.character("Mara", temperament=anxious)
mara.senses("phone")
mara.appraises("phone", as_threat=True, feeling="dread", when="phone > 3")
mara.narrates(voice={"dread": "It's nothing. It's probably nothing."})
story.at("2s", mara.hears("phone", 9))

print(story.run())               # the dashboard

Run it:

python3 my_first.py

2. The core idea, in one character

The my_first.py you just ran is our first character, Mara. Those five lines of intent map onto the four things every SOMA character is made of:

  • senses — a channel onto the world. Mara can hear her phone.
  • appraises — a loop that reads a channel and makes something of it. Mara treats the phone as_threat, and when it rings loudly enough (when="phone > 3") the loop fires and she feels dread.
  • the feeling (a quale) — the feeling itself, which is opaque: the simulation records that dread happened, never what dread is made of. You can count feelings and compare them against what was said, but you cannot open one up. (This is a deliberate stance: the model refuses to pretend it knows the texture of an inner state.)
  • narrates — the confabulating self, the voice in her head. Mara's voice insists It's nothing. It's probably nothing.

Here is the whole thing again, as a self-contained script:

from soma.narrative import Story, anxious

story = Story("first_feeling", span="6s", step="1s", about="a first feeling")
mara = story.character("Mara", temperament=anxious)
mara.senses("phone")
mara.appraises("phone", as_threat=True, feeling="dread", when="phone > 3")
mara.narrates(voice={"dread": "It's nothing. It's probably nothing."})
story.at("2s", mara.hears("phone", 9))
print(story.run())

What a loop actually does

Everything in SOMA is built from loops, and one idea makes them tick, drawn from predictive-processing theory: a loop is always predicting what it will sense, then comparing that prediction to what it actually senses. The mismatch is the error, and what the loop does about the error is the whole game.

Each tick, an appraisal loop:

  1. predicts a value (its prior — what it expects the channel to read),
  2. senses the channel's real value,
  3. computes the error between them, weighted by two dials — precision (trust in the senses) and conviction (trust in the prior), and
  4. if the error clears a firing threshold, the loop fires: it runs its reaction (emitting a feeling, letting the narrator speak) and arbitrates what to do with the surprise. If it trusts its senses more it perceives (revises its belief toward the world); if it trusts its prior more it acts (holds the belief and pushes back on the world instead).

A small error changes nothing — the world matched the prediction, so there is nothing to react to. Only a surprise large enough to clear the threshold makes something happen. That is why Mara feels dread only when the phone crosses her threshold: until the call lands, her calm prediction holds; when it lands, the error spikes, the loop fires, and dread is emitted. Because she is anxious (high conviction, low precision) she acts rather than perceives — she does not revise her sense that something is wrong; she feels it, and narrates over it.

What a temperament sets

A temperament is just a bundle of those dials, named so you never type raw numbers to get a recognizable disposition. anxious runs low precision and high conviction: the prior something is wrong outranks the evidence, so an anxious character reacts to a threat that may not be there. The library ships eight — anxious, stoic, trusting, guarded, volatile, numb, tender, and hollowed — and you can override any single dial per loop (you will see precision=, conviction=, and learn= used that way later). The full table is in §8.

How to read a settle line

The chronicle's settle lines are the loop resolving, tick by tick, and they carry the numbers from the four-step cycle above. Reading one:

2.0s settle appraising_phone sense=9.0 belief=6.0 error=1.65 pi_s=0.55 route=act

means: at 2 seconds the appraising_phone loop sensed 9.0, believed (had predicted) 6.0, so the raw surprise was +3.0, scaled by sensory precision pi_s=0.55 to an error of 1.65 — over the firing threshold, so the loop fired — and it routed to act (held its prior and pushed back) rather than perceive (revise toward the world), because Mara's conviction outranks her precision. That single line is the whole model in motion: predict, sense, weigh, fire, arbitrate.

The generated SOMA (there is no magic underneath)

Those five lines of Python compiled to an ordinary SOMA program. You rarely need to read it, but it is worth seeing once — the high-level library is a convenience, not a separate engine:

// first_feeling -- generated by soma.narrative
// Every construct below was produced from a high-level narrative description;
// it is ordinary SOMA and can be edited, run, sifted, prosed, and perturbed.
@consent("a first feeling")

sim { duration: 6s  dt: 1s }

body Mara_body @cardiac {
  extero phone : Signal baseline 0
}

loop appraising_phone @cardiac {
  prior:      predict(6)
  sense:      phone
  precision:  0.55
  conviction: 0.7
  learn:      0.03
  act {
    emit feel(dread) when phone > 3
  }
}

narrator self subscribes { appraising_phone } confabulates true
  voice { dread: "It's nothing. It's probably nothing." }

stimulus phone { at 2s: 9 }

You can see the loop's prior, precision, and conviction right there — the dials the anxious temperament set. (The learn: 0.03 line is a slow hardening of the prior with use; it does nothing dramatic here, and gets a starring role in §4.3.) Every guarantee of the base language holds: the feeling is opaque, the @consent line is required because the program simulates distress, and the narrator is a separate construct that subscribes to the loop and speaks over it.

3. Two people: coupling

A single character can misread the world. Two characters can misread each other — and that needs one new verb, reads, which creates a coupling. One character reads another's surface (a face, a tone) — never their interior. You can see someone's face; you cannot see their feeling.

from soma.narrative import Story, anxious, guarded

story = Story("a_glance", span="8s", step="1s", about="being seen")
ida = story.character("Ida", temperament=anxious)
theo = story.character("Theo", temperament=guarded)

ida.reads(theo, "face", gain=0.9, lag="1s")     # she reads his face, a beat late
ida.appraises("theo_face", feeling="shame", when="theo_face > 4")
ida.narrates(voice={"shame": "He can see right through me."})

story.at("2s", theo.shows("face", 9))           # Theo's face does something
print(story.run())

reads gives Ida a new channel, theo_face, that tracks Theo's real face with a gain (how strongly it registers) and a lag (how late). When his face rises, the reading crosses her threshold, her loop fires, and she makes shame of it — telling herself he can see through her.

Notice what is not here: as_threat=True. Ida does not go in expecting his face to mean anything (her prior is a flat zero), so when it rises it is a genuine surprise — a large error — and that is what makes the loop fire. Had we marked it a threat, she would have expected the worst, her belief would already sit high, and his real face would match it too closely to register. Small choices in how a character is wired change what they can feel.

That is what makes coupling dramatic rather than telepathic: the reader supplies the meaning, and can be wrong.

4.1 bad_news — the body knows first

The smallest complete person: a woman takes a phone call. It introduces two ideas. feels(from_body=...) reads a feeling off the body rather than straight off the world — dread is what a racing heart means. And drives lets an appraisal reach into physiology: hearing the words drives her heart. Because the feeling follows the heart, and the heart follows the news, the order on the page is: body first, feeling second, and the calm story she tells last of all.

def build():
    story = Story("bad_news", span="8s", step="0.5s",
                  about="simulates acute distress")
    nadia = story.character("Nadia", temperament=anxious)

    # she hears; the words drive her heart -- but only once they land (the guard),
    # so the body's spike is an event in time, not a constant.
    nadia.senses("ear")
    nadia.appraises("ear", as_threat=True, drives="heart", to=118,
                    when="ear > 3", fades_to=70)
    # the feeling is read off the body: dread is what a racing heart means. It
    # follows the heart rather than arriving with the news -- the body knew first.
    nadia.feels("dread", from_body="heart")
    # and the self narrates a composure the body is busy contradicting.
    nadia.narrates(downplaying={
        "dread": "I'm fine. I just need to write this down."})

    # a quiet moment, then the sentence lands, then quiet she cannot return to
    story.at("2s", nadia.hears("ear", 9))
    story.at("5s", nadia.hears("ear", 2))
    return story

Note fades_to=70: a driven channel decays back to rest, so the racing heart is an event with a shape, not a permanent new floor. Here is the dashboard:

4.2 the_look — a misreading that feeds itself

Now both characters have interiors, and they read each other. The trick is shows_on: when Ana feels shame, it shows on her face — which becomes the very signal Ivo reads and makes contempt of. His contempt shows on his face, which deepens her shame. A spiral, and nobody intends any of it. Each also keeps a has_mood — a slow variable that accumulates a feeling and outlasts any single moment.

def build():
    story = Story("the_look", span="12s", step="1s", about="mutual misreading")

    ana = story.character("Ana", temperament=anxious)
    ivo = story.character("Ivo", temperament=guarded)

    # each reads the other's face, one second late
    ana.reads(ivo, "face", gain=1.0, lag="1s")
    ivo.reads(ana, "face", gain=1.0, lag="1s")

    # each makes a feeling of the other's face -- and the feeling SHOWS, so it
    # becomes the very signal the other misreads. shows_on + fades_to closes the
    # loop: the face gives them away, then composes, then gives them away again.
    # The gain is 1.0 (not attenuated): a face that reaches 9 clears the threat-
    # prior by enough to keep firing, so the spiral is mutual rather than
    # one-sided -- both of them misread, both of them escalate.
    ana.appraises("ivo_face", as_threat=True, feeling="shame",
                  shows_on="face", shows_value=9, fades_to=3, when="ivo_face > 4")
    ivo.appraises("ana_face", as_threat=True, feeling="contempt",
                  shows_on="face", shows_value=9, fades_to=3, when="ana_face > 4")

    # each keeps a mood: the evening's residue, rising as the spiral turns
    ana.has_mood("humiliation", fed_by="shame", decay=0.85)
    ivo.has_mood("disdain", fed_by="contempt", decay=0.85)

    ana.narrates(voice={"shame": "He sees straight through me."})
    ivo.narrates(voice={"contempt": "She wants something from me."})

    # one glance sets it off; after that the two faces drive each other
    story.at("2s", ana.shows("face", 9), ivo.shows("face", 9))
    story.at("6s", ana.shows("face", 9), ivo.shows("face", 9))
    return story

4.3 a_marriage — thirty years, and a prior that hardens

This one runs on the biography clock (clock="life", one step per year) and introduces learns. Soren, tender, delights in being surprised by his wife — low conviction plus being wrong equals delight. But learns(0.055) makes the loop harden with use: every confirmation makes his model of her a little more certain, until — with updates=True, stops_seeing=True — the hardened prior outranks his senses and he stops taking her in. Then she is gone, and a second appraisal that still expects=5 (her presence) finds absence, and grief.

def build():
    story = Story("a_marriage", span="30y", step="1y", cadence=True,
                  about="the slow erosion of intimacy, and grief")

    soren = story.character("Soren", temperament=tender, clock="life")
    soren.senses("her_face", baseline=5)
    # low conviction + being wrong = delight; learning turns it to certainty.
    # updates=True: he revises his picture of her. stops_seeing=True: once the
    # hardened prior outranks his senses, he stops taking her in.
    soren.appraises("her_face", feeling="delight_at_error", when="her_face > 1",
                    precision=0.75, conviction=0.2,
                    updates=True, stops_seeing=True)
    soren.learns(0.055)
    # and when her face is gone -- the channel fallen to nothing where a person
    # used to be -- the same body that delighted now grieves. It goes on
    # expecting her (expects=5); finding absence is the error that becomes grief.
    soren.appraises("her_face", feeling="grief", when="her_face < 1",
                    expects=5, precision=0.9, conviction=0.2, learn=0.0)
    soren.narrates(voice={"delight_at_error": "She surprised me. Still.",
                          "grief": "The house is so quiet now."})

    face = arc.wobble(around=5, span="24y", every="2y") + arc.hold(0, at="25y")
    for (t, v) in face:
        story.at(t, soren.hears("her_face", v))
    return story

4.4 the_house — trauma across three generations

The first genuinely large simulation: ninety years, three characters, and a fear that passes down a lineage. It combines everything so far and adds two mechanisms. remembers gives Vera a somatic memory — a flashback keyed to a cue, firing a feeling with no story attached. dissociates_when gives her a supervisor that detaches under overload and slowly repairs.

The transmission works face-to-face: Vera's terror shows_on her face; her daughter Ada reads that face and makes dread of it (and, learning, hardens) — and Ada's dread shows on her face, which her daughter Mira reads as an unease she cannot name. The war reaches the granddaughter as weather.

def build():

4.5 the_diplomat — wanting, and a value broken

Back to one person, but a deep one. Three new ideas arrive together. wants and fears on the same channel create ambivalence: two drives pulling opposite ways, so Rader is drawn to being known and repelled by it at once, and settles nowhere. values gives him a stated principle — I would never lie to her — and the bodily condition under which he betrays it (composing his face). And effortful appraisals must spend a finite attention spotlight (has_attention); when it is exhausted they are starved, which is how a man too busy holding himself together never quite feels his own longing.

def build():

                  about="a man defended against his own longing")

    story.scene("The table", frm="0s", to="6s")
    story.scene("What she asks", frm="7s", to="12s")
    story.scene("The practiced calm", frm="13s", to="18s")

    # he values honesty. shows_on lets the composure register; the value watches.

4.7 the_negotiator — the most complete character

The last of the core examples puts every layer on one person at once. Cass runs hostage negotiations; she is, professionally, the calmest person in any room. The simulation is built to show that the calm is a structure with a cost, and it introduces the two deepest constructs in the library.

Internal parts (part) model a divided psyche. Each part is a sub-agent that reacts to something and bids for consciousness at a salience. A loud protector (the professional, salience 0.95) wins; a quiet exile (the frightened child, salience 0.28) feels real terror that never crosses the threshold into awareness — felt, never known.

The relational self (with_person) makes one person several. Cass runs different precision and conviction with the subject at the door (wary) than with the colleague in her ear (open) — the same body, two selves, indexed by whom she is with.

On top of those sit the constructs you already know: ambivalence about the rapport that would end the standoff, a candor value she breaks with every composed word, a mood that depletes, and a narrator that insists she is fine.

def build():

5.1 craves — a hunger the world can feed and charge for

craves(name, fed_by=, feeling=, erased=) gives a character a want a place can supply and withdraw. The single dial erased decides a hunger's fate, and the sift tells the three fates apart. This is Blade, in the fighting-ring: the ring supplies a rank, and the nightly tide-clean wipes the floor, so the rank he won at dawn is gone by the next tide and he must win it again. Around that one wiped hunger he is built as a whole divided man — a loud, brave protector that speaks, and, under it, the starved animal that never gets heard.

def build_ring():
    """Blade in the Blood. He is 'genuinely, gloriously good' -- quick, brave past
    sense, first over any rail -- and the Articles he swore give him no way to be
    *more* than the bilge-boy. The ring does: it supplies a rank. But the
    tide-clean wipes the fighting-floor every night, so the rank he won at dawn is
    gone by the next tide and he must win it again -- the ring 'sells the feeling
    of a rank and erases the rank while you sleep.'

    He is not a fool or a traitor; he is a divided man. A loud protector -- the
    one who is first through the smoke -- and, under it, the same small starved
    animal Ink carries, the one that has never stopped asking whether he mattered,
    which the injury-scale refused to feed and the ring finally does. He signed
    the Articles and meant it ('if the fleet's ever aground I'll be first in the
    water') and the same week began straining against them. The sift should
    recover all of it: the wiped hunger, the brave self that speaks and the
    starved self that never gets heard, the arc from pride to worthlessness, and
    the fair share he swore and could not live inside."""
    s = Story("blade_in_the_blood", span="22s", step="1s",
              about="a hunger the ring feeds and the tide erases")
    blade = s.character("Blade", temperament=volatile)

    # the wiped hunger -- fed by the ring's rank, erased nightly by the tide-clean
    blade.craves("to_matter", fed_by="rank", feeling="worthlessness",
                 fed_feeling="pride", erased=0.9, threshold=5,
                 says="I'm first through the smoke. What do I get that the bilge-boy doesn't?")

    # the divided self: the brave first-over-the-rail protector is loud; the
    # spare animal that only wants to matter is quiet, and never gets heard.
    blade.part("first-over-the-rail", role="protector", reacts_to="rank",
               feeling="pride", salience=0.9, when="rank > 3",
               says="Wet first, every time. You all saw that.")
    blade.part("the-spare-animal", role="exile", reacts_to="the_scale",
               feeling="worthlessness", salience=0.2, when="the_scale > 3",
               says="Same share. Same vote. As if I were the boy pumping bilge.")

    # the value he swore and strains: a fair share, no upstairs -- and the ache
    # of needing one crosses its own line whenever the scale is put to him.
    blade.values("the_fair_share", says="I signed it and I meant it. First in the water, always.",
                 betrayed_when="the_scale > 5", on_channel="the_scale")

    blade.narrates(voice={
        "worthlessness": "Some of us need there to be an upstairs.",
        "pride": "You built a home with no upstairs. I can't live in it.",
        "self_betrayal": "I signed it and I meant it. And I can't stay.",
    })

    # Under the wiped hunger sits the wound that installed it, the lie it taught,
    # and the need the lie will not let him reach. The fleet treats him as an
    # equal -- the fair share, the equal vote -- which is exactly the evidence his
    # lie must suppress, because if the equal share is enough, he is not surplus,
    # and he cannot believe that. He is not breakable here: met with the evidence,
    # he doubles down and takes the ladder. The tragic arc, and the fleet loses him.
    blade.wounded_by("the injury-scale that priced him in advance, and gave him "
                     "no way to be worth more than the boy pumping bilge",
                     teaches="only-rank-makes-me-real")
    blade.believes("only-rank-makes-me-real",
                   claim="If I'm not ranked above them, I'm the surplus again.",
                   disconfirmed_by="equal_regard", feeling="worthlessness",
                   harms="others", breakable=None,
                   says="Some of us need there to be an upstairs.")
    blade.needs("to-matter-as-an-equal", opposes="only-rank-makes-me-real",
                feeling="worthlessness", fed_feeling="belonging")

    # he wins at dawn (rank supplied), the tide wipes it; and across it all the
    # fair-share scale keeps being held up to him, and keeps not being enough.
    for t in [2, 9, 16]:                 # the crowd's grade, at dawn
        s.at(f"{t}s", blade.hears("rank", 9))
    for t in [5, 12, 19]:                # the tide-clean, by nightfall
        s.at(f"{t}s", blade.hears("rank", 0))
    for t in [4, 8, 11, 15, 18]:         # the Articles' fair scale, put to him
        s.at(f"{t}s", blade.hears("the_scale", 8))

    for t in [3, 7, 10, 13, 17, 20]:
        s.at(f"{t}s", blade.hears("equal_regard", 8))
    return s

5.2 tended_by — co-regulation, presence rather than argument

tended_by(other, calms=) with steadies() on the other models the difference between arguing someone out of a feeling and sitting beside them until it eases. It turns down the trust placed in a distress signal while a steady other is present — without touching the belief. The tended one still sees what they see; they are simply no longer alone with it. This is Topman, whose grief floods, and Rover, who does not argue — he arrives, and stays.

def build_combs():
    """Topman lost his wife and both children in the crush at the Maw and held
    the fleet's course steady from aloft while it happened. His grief floods. The
    Hive offers 'an end to carrying it,' and Ink -- who believes if you make a
    person see the machine they will walk out of it -- argues, and it is exactly
    wrong: 'you cannot argue a person out of the one place their dead have stopped
    dying.' Rover does the other thing. He fills a kit-bag with biscuit, sits down
    in the golden hush, and is a true thing from the true world within reach until
    the true world has a thread in it again.

    So: Topman's grief is not argued away (the belief is never touched); it is
    that, once Rover is present and steady, the trust placed in the alarm falls
    and it stops flooding. Presence, not persuasion -- the book's whole thesis,
    and the recovery-watch the fleet built out of it: no one alone in the dark."""
    s = Story("the_combs", span="16s", step="1s",
              about="grief quieted by a steady presence, not an argument")
    topman = s.character("Topman", temperament=anxious)
    rover = s.character("Rover", temperament=stoic)

    topman.has_body_signal("the_children", baseline=0)
    topman.appraises("the_children", as_threat=True, feeling="grief",
                     when="the_children > 4")
    rover.steadies()                                   # present when he sits down
    topman.tended_by(rover, calms="the_children", strength=0.85)
    topman.narrates(voice={"grief": "In here, the children stopped drowning."})
    rover.narrates(voice={"calm": "Your boat leaked, though."})

    # the grief is there the whole time; Rover comes and sits on the third day.
    for t in range(2, 16):
        s.at(f"{t}s", topman.hears("the_children", 9))
    for t in range(8, 16):
        s.at(f"{t}s", rover.present())
    return s

5.3 holds_with_others — confidence as belief about other people's belief

holds_with_others(name, field_tag=) models a belief whose whole strength is that everyone else is seen to hold it. Its conviction reads a shared field — how much of the group is still holding — via a small core builtin, field(tag), that averages a named channel across the whole cast. While the field is full the belief is unshakeable; when it thins past a tipping point, each holder lets go, which thins the field further, and the whole thing collapses far faster than any evidence could move it.

This is the run on the bank at Greywater. Five creditors hold war-paper "sound" only because the others do. One of them, Venn, actually reads the paper — an appraisal that checks and updates — and when the arithmetic comes up rotten his own reading (not any outside shock) tips him out of the belief. Quill reads the same rot and holds anyway, because knowing is not what moves a market. Then confidence, which stood for days against the truth, cannot stand one hour against the sight of one man acting on it.

def build_greywater():
    """The climax's engine. War-paper is 'sound' because every house is seen to
    treat it as sound -- confidence, in the book's exact sense: belief about other
    people's belief. Truth alone cannot move it ('everyone knew the house was
    rotten... each was waiting to see what the others would do'). What moves it is
    one man, Venn, willing to be first at counting in public.

    So the run here is not sprung by an outside shock -- it is sprung by a
    character. Venn actually reads the paper (an appraisal that checks, and
    updates), and when the arithmetic comes up rotten his own reading -- not the
    crowd's -- tips him out of the belief. The other four never read the paper at
    all; they read each other, and hold until they see Venn go. He does not do it
    for anyone; he does it because he can count, and because he is the one man in
    the hall willing to be first at counting out loud. Then confidence, which
    stood six days against the truth, cannot stand one hour against the sight of
    one man acting on it: nothing, nothing, then everything at once."""
    s = Story("greywater", span="20s", step="1s",
              about="confidence, and the one man willing to count first")
    names = ["Venn", "Marl", "Osset", "Perrit", "Quill"]
    banks = [s.character(n, temperament=guarded) for n in names]
    for b in banks:
        b.holds_with_others(
            "paper_is_sound", field_tag="holds", believing=10,
            says="Sound as the day is long. Everyone says so.")

    # Venn alone reads the actual paper, and checks it (updates). When the rot is
    # plain, his reading drives his own doubt -- and that, not any outside shock,
    # is what unmoors the whole hall. He is the first mover because he counts.
    venn = banks[0]
    venn.has_body_signal("the_paper", baseline=0)
    venn.appraises("the_paper", feeling="foreboding", updates=True,
                   when="the_paper > 5",
                   drives="paper_is_sound_doubt", to=1)
    venn.narrates(voice={
        "foreboding": "They sell to both sides. So some day they are on the other side of me too.",
    })

    # Quill is the tragedy of the hall. He reads the very same rot -- he KNOWS --
    # and holds anyway, because knowing is not the thing that moves a market:
    # belief about other people's belief is. He does not drive his own doubt; his
    # grip only breaks when he sees Venn's break, on the field, with the rest.
    quill = banks[-1]
    quill.has_body_signal("the_paper", baseline=0)
    quill.appraises("the_paper", feeling="foreboding", when="the_paper > 5")
    quill.narrates(voice={
        "foreboding": "I know. I can read. And I am so tired. Sound as the day is long.",
    })
    # both men have the rot in front of them the whole time; one counts it, one
    # only reads it and waits.
    for t in [4, 6, 8, 10]:
        s.at(f"{t}s", venn.hears("the_paper", 8))
    for t in [4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14]:
        s.at(f"{t}s", quill.hears("the_paper", 8))
    return s

5.4 hollowed — the subtracted self

The eighth temperament, hollowed, sets both dials near the floor: little of the world lands, and little is firmly held. It is the disposition of the one the world has slowly emptied — a keeper of names worn down to her rounds, a captain in the mist who can no longer tell the shown from the so. It is distinct from numb (which still holds a prior); a hollowed self holds almost nothing, so it neither floods nor defends — it simply thins. Reach for it exactly as any other temperament: story.character("Ita", temperament=hollowed).

5.5 wounded_by / believes / needs — the wound, the lie, and the arc

The deepest layer of character, and the one the craft of fiction is most explicit about (Truby's ghost → lie → need; McKee's "dimension means contradiction"): a formative wound teaches a false lie the character adopts to survive it; the lie generates the conscious want while defending against a buried need; and the story is the collision between them. Getting the want never heals the character — only seeing the lie does, and whether they see it is the arc.

SOMA models this on its own predictive-processing machinery. The lie is a high-conviction loop that reads the very evidence that would disconfirm it and, because conviction outranks that evidence, suppresses it — which is exactly the mechanism of self-deception. What decides the arc is a core primitive, overwhelm: the weight of the suppressed evidence accumulates, and if it crosses a threshold the belief can no longer hold and is forced to perceive — a mechanistic self-revelation.

The threshold is not a number you have to guess. By default (breakable=True) the lie breaks automatically, and when it breaks is derived from the belief's own strengths: the accumulated disconfirming surprise has to exceed roughly the belief's conviction divided by how much it trusts that evidence. So a lie held harder resists longer, a lie that distrusts its evidence resists longer, and — because each suppression hardens the belief (learn) — a lie that has been denied for a long time is harder to break than a fresh one, exactly as entrenched convictions are in life. You set how strongly the lie is held; the moment of self-revelation emerges.

Here is the same character wired two ways — the only difference is how hard the lie is held:

from soma.narrative import Story, volatile

def arc(conviction, breakable=True):
    s = Story("arc", span="18s", step="1s", about="a lie and its arc")
    b = s.character("B", temperament=volatile)
    b.wounded_by("named the surplus as a boy", teaches="only-rank")
    b.believes("only-rank",
               claim="If I'm not ranked above them, I'm the surplus again.",
               disconfirmed_by="equal_regard", feeling="worthlessness",
               harms="others", conviction=conviction, breakable=breakable,
               says="Some of us need there to be an upstairs.")
    b.needs("to-matter-as-an-equal", opposes="only-rank",
            feeling="worthlessness", fed_feeling="belonging")
    for t, v in [("3s", 6), ("6s", 7), ("9s", 8), ("12s", 9), ("15s", 9), ("17s", 9)]:
        s.at(t, b.hears("equal_regard", v))   # the fleet's equal regard, escalating
    return s

Conviction is the dial that separates the novel's own cast. Blade (the_unmooring.py ring) runs the unbreakable lie — if I'm not ranked above them I'm the surplus again — and takes the ring's ladder; his need to matter as an equal aches to the end and is never met. Ink (fleet) and the Coat hold breakable versions of the same wound and are seen: Ink's lie that he matters only as the indispensable hand is overwhelmed at the founding (he refuses the pen), and the Coat's that he is only the house's number breaks at the gate, when his own unbought acts finally outweigh it. The Provost (selm) holds his lie hardest of the four (the highest conviction — thirty years of doctrine), so even under the true sums held up to his face it resists the longest and breaks only at the very last: the self-revelation that comes too late to undo anything, which is its own kind of tragedy. None of those four moments is a hand-set number; each falls out of how hard the lie is held against how strong the evidence gets. 5.6 Prediction — from modelling a character to forecasting one

Everything so far models a character: you specify the pieces and SOMA computes what follows. A stronger test of a character library is whether it makes positive predictions — whether it can tell you something you did not put in. Cognitive science draws exactly this line between a simulation (which replays its inputs) and a prediction (which must generate an effect under conditions it was never given, and can therefore be wrong). Two of the library's tools cross it.

Predict the lie from the wound. Instead of stating a character's lie, you can give only the wound — an unmet core need — and how they cope with it, and let the library forecast the belief. The mapping is schema therapy's: an unmet need becomes a core self-belief, and a coping style (surrender, avoidance, overcompensation) turns it into a characteristic lie, want, and behaviour. The prediction is that the same wound, coped three ways, is three different people:

the wound: an unmet need for worth (Defectiveness / Shame)

surrender          "I am the one who can be done without."
                   wants to be tolerated, to take up little; harms self
avoidance          "If I never test it, no one can find me wanting."
                   wants to not be measured; harms self
overcompensation   "If I am not ranked above them, I am the surplus again."
                   wants rank, an upstairs, to be visibly above others; harms others

The overcompensation branch is, word for word, the lie the novel gives Blade — so adopts("worth", "overcompensation", ...) rebuilds his tragic arc from the wound alone, and the surrender and avoidance branches predict the unwritten brothers he might have been. That is a positive prediction: structure derived, not asserted.

Forecast a response the author never wrote. Given a finished character, predict() runs the same machinery on a situation that is not in the timeline and reports what the character does with it — suppress it, take it in, or break. This is the counterfactual a novelist runs in their head when a character starts to feel real ("what would she do if…"); here it is falsifiable, because the model must actually produce the effect:

Prediction — Blade faced with equal_regard at 9 (never scripted):
  they SUPPRESS it — the belief holds, the evidence is defended against. No change.
  felt: worthlessness.

Prediction — Ink faced with kept_for_nothing at 9 (never scripted):
  it BREAKS the lie 'only the needed matter' at ~3s — a self-revelation.
  felt: worthlessness.

Blade suppresses even full equal regard; Ink, shown he is kept for nothing, breaks. The library tells the two apart on input neither was scripted to meet — which is the whole difference between describing a character and predicting one. And the prediction can be made sharp: tipping_point() returns the least evidence that turns each lie —

Ink    on 'kept_for_nothing': breaks once evidence >= 3.0
Selm   on 'the_margin': breaks once evidence >= 6.0
Blade  on 'equal_regard': never breaks in [0, 9] — the lie is kept

Selm's doctrine, held hardest of the cast, needs the strongest evidence before it turns; Blade's never turns at all. Neither number was written down anywhere — both are forecasts the model stands behind, and either could be shown wrong by running it. That is what it means for the library to predict rather than merely model.

6. The five views

Every simulation can be looked at five ways. Use whichever fits the question.

A sixth tool is not a view but an experiment: story.perturb("dial=value") changes one number, re-runs, and diffs the findings, so you can ask whether a mechanism is load-bearing. If a finding vanishes when you sever a coupling or flatten a learning rate, that mechanism was carrying the story — as we saw prove the inheritance in the_house and the structural self-deception in the_negotiator.

7. The vocabulary, in one place

Everything in this tutorial is built from a small set of verbs on a Character. Roughly from simple to deep:

  • senses(channel) / has_body_signal(channel) — a channel onto the world, or the body
  • appraises(channel, as_threat=, drives=, feeling=, when=, updates=, stops_seeing=, effortful=, shows_on=, fades_to=, ...) — a loop that reads a channel and reacts
  • feels(quale, from_body=) — read a feeling off the body
  • narrates(voice=/downplaying=) — the confabulating self
  • reads(other, surface=, gain=, lag=) — a coupling: read another's surface
  • has_mood(name, fed_by=, decay=) — a slow variable that outlasts a feeling
  • learns(rate) — make appraisals harden with use
  • remembers(name, cued_by=, evokes=) — a somatic memory / flashback
  • dissociates_when(appraisal=, exceeds=, repair_after=) — a supervisor that detaches under overload
  • has_attention(capacity=) + effortful=True — a finite spotlight that deliberate loops spend
  • wants(channel) / fears(channel) — drives; both on one channel = ambivalence
  • values(name, says=, betrayed_when=) — a principle, and the bodily condition of its breach
  • part(name, role=, reacts_to=, feeling=, salience=) — an internal part that bids for consciousness
  • with_person(other, feeling=, precision=, conviction=) — a different self for a specific person
  • craves(name, fed_by=, feeling=, erased=) — a hunger the world can feed and wipe (three fates: fed, wiped, unfed)
  • tended_by(other, calms=) / steadies() / present() — co-regulation: presence lowers a distress's precision, not its belief
  • holds_with_others(name, field_tag=, shocked_at=) — confidence as belief about other people's belief, and its cascade
  • wounded_by(ghost, teaches=, cued_by=, evokes=) — the formative wound (the Ghost), and the lie it taught
  • believes(lie, claim=, disconfirmed_by=, feeling=, harms=, conviction=, breakable=) — the Lie: a belief that suppresses its own disconfirming evidence and either breaks (a self-revelation, whose moment breakable=True derives automatically from conviction vs. evidence) or, held hard enough (breakable=None), only hardens
  • needs(truth, opposes=, feeling=, fed_feeling=) — the Need: the salve to the lie, not fed by the want, met only when the lie is seen
  • adopts(unmet, coping, disconfirmed_by=) — predict the lie from the wound: an unmet need + a coping style (surrender / avoidance / overcompensation) forecasts the belief, want, and its grip (schema therapy)
  • predict(who, {channel: value}) — forecast a character's response to a situation never scripted (suppress / take-in / break); tipping_point(who, channel) gives the least evidence that turns the lie
  • predict_lie(unmet, coping) (module-level) — inspect the predicted lie for any wound + coping, without building a character

The eight temperaments (each a preset of the two dials, some with a learning rate): anxious, stoic, trusting, guarded, volatile, numb, tender, hollowed. Override any single dial per loop with precision=, conviction=, learn=.

The sift patterns the WINNOW-S panel and story.sift() report include: retained residual, confabulation gap (graded, and it names the denied feeling), precision pathology, delight in error, a mood that sours/lifts, a turn in how they meet the world, body remembers alone, rupture and repair, attention starved, never ignited, the thing they both want and fear (ambivalence), the value the body broke, the feeling the whole self defends against, the person they became, a hunger the world keeps wiping / keeps fed / will not feed, held in the dark, the run, the lie seen / the lie kept (the arc), and the need met / a need the want never feeds (the want/need collision).

And on the Story: character and scene (declare people and label spans of time), at (place an event at a time) and over (spread an arc shape across the timeline), run / sift / characterize / prose (the views), perturb (the change-one-dial experiment), and source (dump the generated SOMA).

8. Why this yields novelistic insight

The recurring move in every example is the same: two mechanisms that can disagree. The narrator says one thing; the body's record says another. A value is held in words and broken in the flesh. A part bids for awareness and is never heard. A hunger is fed by one supply and wiped by another. A person is one self here and another there.

None of those gaps is authored as a conclusion. You write the parts — the dials, the couplings, the drives, the hungers — and the gap emerges from running them; then the sift finds it and names it. That is why the output can surprise the author: you did not write she defends against terror; you wrote a loud protector and a quiet child, and the sift discovered which feeling never got through. You did not write the same hunger saves one brother and ruins the other; you set one erased=0.0 and the other erased=0.9, and the contrast fell out.

Characterization, in a novel, is precisely this: not what a person says about themselves, but the distance between that and what they do. SOMA makes that distance a measurable, inspectable thing — and hands it back to you as a dashboard, a portrait, a prose passage, and a ranked list of the moments that matter.

Start with bad_news, change a temperament, and watch the findings move. Then open the_unmooring and watch a whole cast assemble from the same handful of verbs. That is the whole method.
